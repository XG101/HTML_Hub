Modding Website to Look Better

The goal is to look through a website and change tags and elements through the inspect feature

Before Changes

Here we have a site that has a couple of things wrong and annoying about it. First, one of the headers tries to be special and have part of the message be typed in and out every second which for a reading purpose makes it very annoying for the user to read.

Second the buttons for the top and slideshow are meant to be the same yet fail color coordination. Can be very confusing for the user if they read through the page and just remember by color what the button is suppose to be.

On the topic of color in my opinion the colors here are not bad but can be redone. I personally not a fan of white being the primary color and much prefer a darker color like light gray or the blue that is used for this site instead. This also goes with the fonts, while it is not horriable there are better fonts out there to use.

My Final Result

Here is my take on the site. Changed the colors for blue to be the primary and white to be the third color or 10% rule from class. I removed the 'fancy' header script and changed the font to mainly Arial and Times New Roman.

Color Changing

What I did first was the color changing to help get familar with the inspect feature. I would go through the tag sections and look for specific elements to change, in this case anything that could help with changing the section background or a header/paragraph text color.

Font Changing

Next order of business was to locate the font family. Like the colors I went through the elements and looked for the font families. While they mainly had the same stuff, the fonts I noticed would only change by changing the font type that was in the quotations.

Header Changing

Lastly I went to the header to force the fancy feature off. When I located the element I found it had two header tags, one scripted the other not scripted. The simplest solution was for me to copy and paste the text from the scripted element and added to the note scripted element. Afterwards I deleted the empty scripted element.
